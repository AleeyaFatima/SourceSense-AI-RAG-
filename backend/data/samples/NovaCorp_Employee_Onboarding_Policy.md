# NovaCorp Employee Onboarding Policy

**Document ID:** HR-POL-2024-003
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2025
**Department:** Human Resources
**Approved By:** Sarah Mitchell, VP of People Operations

## Purpose

This policy establishes the standard onboarding process for all new employees joining NovaCorp. A structured onboarding experience ensures new hires become productive quickly, understand company culture and expectations, and have access to the tools and resources they need from day one.

## Scope

This policy applies to all full-time employees, part-time employees, and contractors with engagements exceeding 90 days. Temporary workers and interns follow a modified onboarding track outlined in Appendix B.

## Pre-Arrival (Before Day 1)

The hiring manager and HR coordinator must complete the following at least 5 business days before the new employee's start date:

1. Send the offer letter and employment agreement for electronic signature via DocuSign.
2. Create the employee's NovaCorp account (email, Slack, Jira, Confluence, GitHub).
3. Order and ship equipment: laptop (MacBook Pro 14" for engineering, MacBook Air for non-engineering), monitor, keyboard, and mouse.
4. Assign a buddy from the same team who has been at NovaCorp for at least 6 months.
5. Schedule the first-week calendar with orientation sessions, team introductions, and 1:1 meetings.
6. Add the new hire to relevant Slack channels: #general, #team-[name], #announcements, and #new-hires.

## Day 1

The first day focuses on logistics, introductions, and setting up the work environment.

**Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM):**
- Welcome session with HR (30 minutes): benefits enrollment, payroll setup, company policies overview.
- IT setup session (60 minutes): laptop configuration, VPN access, password manager setup (1Password), MFA enrollment.
- Office tour or virtual workspace orientation (30 minutes).
- Meet the buddy for coffee or virtual chat (30 minutes).

**Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
- Team lunch or virtual team meeting (60 minutes).
- 1:1 with hiring manager (45 minutes): role expectations, 30-60-90 day goals, communication preferences.
- Self-paced reading: company handbook, team wiki, product documentation.

## Week 1

By the end of the first week, the new employee should:

- Have all tools and systems access configured and working.
- Understand the team's current projects and priorities.
- Complete all compliance training modules in the NovaCorp Learning Portal (information security, anti-harassment, data privacy).
- Have their first small task or contribution assigned.
- Complete a 15-minute check-in with HR to address any questions or concerns.

## 30-60-90 Day Framework

### Days 1-30: Learn

The new employee focuses on understanding the product, codebase (for engineers), processes, and team dynamics. Key milestones:

- Complete product deep-dive sessions with the product manager.
- Shadow at least 3 customer calls or support interactions.
- Submit their first pull request or deliverable (for technical roles).
- Review the team's OKRs and understand how their role contributes.

### Days 31-60: Contribute

The employee begins taking ownership of tasks and contributing independently. Key milestones:

- Own at least one feature, project, or initiative.
- Present work to the team in a sprint review or team meeting.
- Provide feedback on the onboarding process to HR.
- Complete a mid-point check-in with the hiring manager to assess progress against 30-60-90 goals.

### Days 61-90: Own

The employee operates independently and begins to influence team direction. Key milestones:

- Deliver a completed project or feature independently.
- Participate in planning and estimation for upcoming work.
- Identify and propose at least one process improvement.
- Complete the formal 90-day review with the hiring manager and HR.

## Benefits Enrollment

New employees must complete benefits enrollment within 30 days of their start date. Available benefits include:

- Health insurance: medical, dental, and vision through Anthem Blue Cross.
- 401(k) with 4% company match (eligible after 90 days).
- Unlimited PTO with a minimum of 15 days per year recommended.
- $1,500 annual learning and development stipend.
- $500 home office setup stipend (remote employees).
- 16 weeks paid parental leave.
- Mental health support through Headspace and Talkiatry partnerships.

## Remote Employee Considerations

For employees working remotely:

- Equipment is shipped to their home address 3-5 business days before their start date.
- All Day 1 sessions are conducted via Zoom with cameras on.
- The buddy should schedule daily 15-minute check-ins during the first week, tapering to weekly by month two.
- Remote employees are invited to the quarterly in-person team offsite (travel and accommodation covered by NovaCorp).

## Compliance

All new employees must complete the following mandatory training within their first 14 days:

- Information Security Awareness (45 minutes)
- Anti-Harassment and Discrimination (30 minutes)
- Data Privacy and GDPR Compliance (30 minutes)
- Code of Conduct acknowledgment (15 minutes)

Failure to complete mandatory training within the 14-day window will be escalated to the employee's manager and VP.

## Feedback and Continuous Improvement

NovaCorp conducts an anonymous onboarding survey at 30, 60, and 90 days. Survey results are reviewed monthly by the People Operations team and used to improve the onboarding experience. In 2024, onboarding satisfaction scores averaged 4.3 out of 5, with "buddy program" and "clear 30-60-90 goals" cited as the most valuable elements.
